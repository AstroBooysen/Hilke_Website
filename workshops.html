August 2026 · Bremen

Augmented Democracies. How AI Can Empower Political Decision-Making by the People and for the People

IJCAI 2026 · Hilke Brockmann and Ivan Yamshchikov

This summer, Hilke Brockmann is co-organizing a workshop at IJCAI 2026 in Bremen titled “Augmented Democracies. How AI Can Empower Political Decision-Making by the People and for the People”. The workshop explores how artificial intelligence can be used to strengthen democratic decision-making rather than undermine it.

Democratic political regimes are under pressure from declining public trust, polarization, and disinformation, while simultaneously facing external competition from authoritarian systems that increasingly deploy AI for centralized control and persuasion. At the same time, recent advances in artificial intelligence — especially large language models (LLMs) — have created new technical capabilities to support democratic deliberation at scale.

A core challenge in democratic theory is the deliberative trilemma: it is difficult to achieve simultaneously broad participation, high-quality deliberation, and political equality. This workshop asks whether purpose-built AI systems can help relax that trilemma.

Traditional democratic institutions typically optimize for only one or two of these dimensions. Recent AI research suggests that carefully designed systems may help synthesize common ground across large and diverse groups, structure argument exchange, and enable multilingual participation.

The workshop brings together researchers from AI, HCI, political science, computational social science, and digital democracy practice to critically assess the state of the art and collaboratively design next-generation AI systems that strengthen democratic decision-making by the people and for the people.
