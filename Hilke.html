About

Hilke Brockmann
Professor of Sociology

School of Business, Social & Decision Sciences

CV

Hilke Brockmann has a multi-disciplinary background in the social sciences. Her research focuses on things that matter to people — aging, happiness, inequality, and new data.

She studies large social groups — the elderly, the educated and uneducated, the haves and the have-nots, migrants, and women. Drawing on new research methodologies, she explores the motives and behaviors of people and their (un)intended consequences.

She uses and combines innovative datasets and cutting-edge data science models to study when and why people are happy, what drives the (un)acceptance of economic inequalities, what defines successful migration, and how people can better participate politically.
