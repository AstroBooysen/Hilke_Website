About Encyclopedia of Happiness, Quality of Life, and Subjective Well-Being

This volume brings together international perspectives on happiness, quality of life, and subjective well-being. It offers an interdisciplinary resource for readers interested in how individuals and societies experience well-being, how these ideas are measured, and why they matter for social research and public life.

The encyclopedia covers core concepts, methods, and debates across the study of subjective well-being, drawing attention to the social, cultural, political, and institutional conditions that shape how people evaluate their lives.

Designed as a reference work for scholars, students, and general readers, the book provides a broad entry point into one of the most important fields in contemporary social science.
